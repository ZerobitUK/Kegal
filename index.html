How to Practice

Kegel exercises strengthen your pelvic floor muscles, supporting bladder, bowel, and sexual health.

  1. Identify: Find your pelvic floor muscles by stopping the flow of urine mid-stream (briefly). Do not pull in your stomach or squeeze your buttocks.
  2. Contract: Squeeze and lift these inner muscles when the circle expands and says Contract. Keep breathing normally.
  3. Relax: Completely release the muscles to rest when the circle shrinks and says Relax. Perfect relaxation is as important as the contraction!
